I Love to Cook

Cooking is one of my very favorite ways to enjoy myself, take care of friends and family, and feel like I’m doing something healthy and worthwhile. I cook almost every night of the week. If I find myself eating takeout or going out to a restaurant more than once a week, I tend to feel almost guilty.

While in the wintertime I enjoy cooking things that take all day, like stews, I’m also an all-seasons griller and barbecuer. There’s something about cooking over an open fire that I just cannot resist. I love to grill fish, chicken, pork, and beef (and the occasional sliced squash or portabella mushroom for my vegetarian friends). But my real passion is for barbecue: slowly smoking pork shoulders, pork or beef ribs, sausages, or beef brisket for hours and hours. That’s especially enjoyable in the warmer spring and summer months, but I cook outdoors regardless of the weather: beer-can chickens in the middle of a polar vortex, or smoked sauerkraut and bratwurst, knackwurst, and Thüringer in the middle of a daylong rainstorm.
